Singing Demos for HiFiSinger

Authors:

Microsoft STC Asia & Microsoft Research Asia



Abstract:

Singing voice synthesis has distinctive characteristics compared with speaking voice synthesis, such as large sampling rate for high-fidelity voice, large range of pitch and long duration due to the nature of singing, which throw great challenges for singing modeling. Different from previous work that directly adopt the pipeline from text to speech on singing voice synthesis, in this paper, we develop MicroSinger, an improved neural singing voice synthesis system to address the distinctive challenges in singing. Specifically, 1) We adopt FastSpeech as the acoustic model and Parallel WaveGAN as the neural vocoder to ensure fast training and inference speed while high voice quality. 2) To handle large range of frequencies caused by high sampling rate in singing voices (e.g., 48kHz vs. 24kHz in speaking voices) and model high-frequency details for high-fidelity singing voices, we propose a novel multi-frequency GAN (MF-GAN) on mel-spectrogram generation, which splits the full 80-dimensional mel-frequency into multiple sub-bands (e.g., low, middle and high frequency bands) and models each sub-band with separate discriminators. 3) To model large range of pitch in singing voices, we use fundamental frequency (F0) and voiced/unvoiced (V/UV) to better model pitch in the vocoder, and also carefully study the window/hop size in STFT (short-time Fourier transform) to better align with the range of pitch. 4) To model long duration on vowels in singing voices, we increase the length of audio clip and enlarge the receptive field in the vocoder. Experiment results show that MicroSinger synthesizes singing voices with much higher quality than previous system. More ablation studies verify the effectiveness of each design in MicroSinger.
